The First 25 Years

I grew up in the only major city in eastern Washington state, called Spokane. I am the younger of two siblings, my brother being my senior by about 4 years. My mom worked as a nurse(now retired) at a major hospital in town, while my dad worked (again, retired) selling natural gas to large consumers such as paper plants, hospitals, etc.
I was an above average student, but always found time for friends and sports in addition to the textbook. I played trombone starting in 5th grade, and band was a large part of my life well into undergrad. In high school, video games were a big part of my life, as Halo 3 and Rock Band with friends were everyday activities. After high school graduation in 2009, I attended Washington State University for a year, learning plenty about why I dislike Pullman(the campus town) in addition to my subject matter. By Thanksgiving 2009, I knew that a change was required and I transferred to the University of Portland, a small catholic university on the north bank of the Wilamette. I completed a B.S. degree in Civil Engineering, and focusing on geotechnical engineering. After graduation in 2014, I took a year off to work and travel. I was accepted to the University of Washington(Seattle) masters program for geotechnical engineering, which I took on and completed in 2016.

Working Life

Ive been fortunate to have some great jobs along the way. The following is a breakdown, from first, to current.
  1. Lifeguard,Parks Department, Spokane WA
  2. Lifeguard, A Golf and Country Club, Spokane WA
  3. Pavement Rater, Spokane Streets, Spokane, WA
  4. Geotechnical Intern, Geotech Engineering Firm, Spokane, WA
  5. Geotechnical Engineer I, Large Environmental Firm, Seattle, WA
  6. Field Engineer, Small specialty drilling contractor, Various

After grad school, I took on my first real engineering job. The salary was low, but I worked hard designing shoring systems for excavations. After a year or so, I decided it was time to try getting back to my hometown of Spokane, so started applying in the area. I hired on at a drilling contractor that was based in Spokane, but it was a traveling job. I assisted with project management tasks for 6 months in upstate New York, followed by a 4 year stint in eastern San Diego county. This employer specialized in helicopter supported construction, which made for made for some interesting work. I flew in helicopters, worked under a sky-crane (THEY ARE HUUUGE!!, see below), and have worked through some tough challenges to accomplish construction in such remote areas.
Along the way, I met a fabulous human, a lawyer who lived a couple hours north of me. So every weekend, I would drive the company car the 2 hours north to go on a date, and then stay the weekend, and now stay together for life. I proposed to Shelby in late September of last year, and we are scheduled to wed in July of this year, shortly after my Coding Temple coding bootcamp concludes.

Soccer

Growing up, I played tons of soccer. I became the regular starter in goal, and once that happened became a goalie for life. I have not played in a few years, but would love to continue playing and plan to join a mens league as life allows. After falling in love with the game and not playing as frequently, I still wanted to witness some of the magic of the beautiful game. I stumbled onto a smaller team from Wales that had just been promoted, and I loved their emblem/crest. That was it, and I was hooked. I still wake up early on Saturdays to watch Swansea City, win lose or draw. We have an exciting team that my see a return to the premier league under an exciting new manager and a young and improving side.
